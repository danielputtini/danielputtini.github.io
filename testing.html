Leveraged Buyout (LBO)

A type of M&A deal where the acquirer uses a great amount of debt (70-75%) to finance an acquisition while using the target's cash flows as collateral

Financial sponsors can profit from an LBO through 3 different effects:

  1. Deleveraging

    2. Basically repaying the debt acquired as much as possible and, after a few years, re-selling for the same EBITDA multiple

  2. Arbitrage

    3. Betting that the market for the target will be better after a few years and that you will be able to sell it for a higher EBITDA multiple

  3. Growth

    4. Very common for Private Equity firms, where they basically get inside managing the firm and try to improve operations, increasing their EBITDA itself. And then selling it
